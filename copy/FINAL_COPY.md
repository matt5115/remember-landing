## Headline Chosen

**Option 2: "Your brain is full. Siri's got this."**

### Why this one wins

- Two short sentences. Rhythm snaps. Impossible to misread.
- "Your brain is full" = instant validation. Zero tech-creep, zero scary AI vibes.
- "Siri's got this" = relief + warm authority. It's a friend saying it, not a product claiming it.
- Cheeky without being snarky. Mom-energy without being condescending.
- "Finally" tone implied without saying the word — hits the #1 emotional driver (relief) immediately.
- Does NOT mention memory in a weird clinical way. Does NOT hint at "your phone is watching you."

**Rejected:** Option 1 ("Your iPhone knows something it shouldn't") — too creepy per brief.

---

## Subheadline

"Finally, a place to dump every name, date, and 'don't let me forget this' moment — and actually not forget it."

- Mirrors the exact internal monologue of the target. The phrase "don't let me forget this" is literally what she says to herself all day.
- "Finally" as the opener signals relief without being the headline itself.
- Light, conversational, sounds like a text message.

## Color Palette Chosen

**Palette 1: Electric Cobalt + Warm Bone + Coral**

```
Hero/sections: #1E3FE0  (Electric Cobalt)
Background:    #F5F0E8  (Warm Bone)
CTA buttons:   #FF5733  (Coral — all primary actions)
Badges:        #FFCF00  (Electric Yellow)
Text:          #1A1A2E  (Near-black navy)
```

### Why this palette

- Cobalt = smart, digital, iOS-native — reads "tech you can trust" without being cold or masculine.
- Warm bone = softer than white, feels like a warm cup of coffee, not a SaaS dashboard.
- Coral CTA = maximum contrast against cobalt, warm, action-oriented, never ignored.
- Yellow badges = fun, sticker energy, TikTok-native, not precious.
- Avoids: sage green (generic wellness 2021), pure white (flat, boring), any grey alternating sections.

## Section Copy Notes

### Problem Section
Tone: empathetic, not shaming. Three enemies named: mom brain, mental load, failed apps.
Closer: "You're not bad at remembering. The system failed you." — reframes guilt as absolution.

### What You Get
Six items, all written as outcomes + use cases. No "Chapter 1: Introduction" table of contents energy.
Price badge in cobalt: $37, prominent, no hiding.

### How It Works
Three steps only. Copy written for a mom who has 90 seconds to skim.
Step 2 includes "You'll know it worked when she repeats it back" — concrete verification beats vague promises.

### Upsell
"Want it first?" framing — not pushy, FOMO-lite, optional always acknowledged.
$17 add-on at checkout (Lemon Squeezy order bump territory).

### Guarantee
"30-Day 'It Works or You're Out' Guarantee" — named, bold, personality-forward.
"Refund the same day. No fine print. No forms. No guilt." — specificity removes fear.
