# TikTok Creative Brief — *Remember*

**Product:** Remember — $37 iOS 27 Siri Memory Setup Guide for iPhones
**Audience:** Women 30–44, moms, TikTok-native, purchase-ready, mentally overloaded
**Core Rule:** NEVER open with setup. Always open with Siri recalling something instantly. Curiosity drives clicks.
**Product Promise:** Your iPhone's built-in Siri (iOS 27) can remember facts, names, codes, and details for you — and recall them instantly, hands-free, out loud. You just have to set it up right.

---

## 1. Five TikTok Hook Variants

---

### HOOK 1 — Disbelief

> "I asked my phone the WiFi password and it just… told me. Out loud."

Works because it's specific, mundane, and instantly relatable — every mom has gone hunting for that password. The "just… told me" pause creates genuine disbelief that stops the scroll.

---

### HOOK 2 — Relatability

> "I have asked my husband for our kids' doctor's name no fewer than forty times. I will never ask him again."

Works because it names an embarrassingly real moment without shame — this audience lives it. The payoff lands as quiet triumph, not tech-brag.

---

### HOOK 3 — Secret

> "There's a setting on your iPhone that most people don't know exists — and it basically gives Siri a memory."

Works because "most people don't know" is a genuine pattern interrupt for anyone who considers themselves savvy. It positions the viewer as someone about to be let in on something.

---

### HOOK 4 — Question

> "Can your iPhone actually remember things for you? Like — names, codes, random stuff you always forget?"

Works because it's a direct question the audience is already asking themselves. The casual "like — names, codes" signals she's going to answer it for real, not in a tech-tutorial way.

---

### HOOK 5 — Story-Based

> "Okay so I'm standing in the school pickup line, and I just said 'Hey Siri, what's our insurance policy number?' And she read it back to me. I stood there like a person."

Works because it's a scene with stakes, specificity, and a relatable setting (school pickup). "I stood there like a person" is funny in a way that signals she's one of us, not a tech person.

## 2. Full 30-Second UGC Ad Script

**Setting:** Kitchen — natural light, slightly cluttered counter in background, coffee mug nearby
**Creator:** Real woman, 32–38, clean-girl casual, no ring light, filmed on iPhone
**Spoken word target:** ~95 words

---

**[0–3s] [ACTION CUE: Hold phone in front of you at counter level, Siri active on screen. Look up at camera mid-interaction — genuine, slightly amused face.]**
*"I asked my phone the WiFi password and it just… told me. Out loud."*

---

**[3–8s] [ACTION CUE: Set phone face-down on counter. Turn to face camera fully. Casual lean on the counter — conversational, not presenting.]**
*"I've been typing that password into devices for three years. Three years. And I just… asked Siri and she said it back to me."*

---

**[8–14s] [ACTION CUE: Gesture loosely with hands — like you're explaining to a friend, not demoing a product.]**

**[14–20s] [ACTION CUE: Slight pause. Pick up your mug. Casual sip — let the idea land before continuing.]**
*"I found this guide — it's called Remember — and it walks you through the exact setup. It took me maybe twenty minutes. And now I will never forget a doctor's name, a parking level, or a password again."*

---

**[20–26s] [ACTION CUE: Put the mug down. Look directly into camera — warm, direct, like you're texting this to your group chat.]**

---

**[26–28s] [ACTION CUE: Small smile, relaxed — no big energy, no sell-face.]**
*"If your brain is full and your iPhone is just sitting there, this is worth it."*

---

**[28–30s] [ACTION CUE: Point casually downward — toward the link. Keep eye contact.]**

## 3. AI Influencer Persona Spec

This is a reference document for building an AI-generated TikTok creator persona to represent the *Remember* product in UGC-style video ads.

---

### Identity

**Name:** Mara
**Age:** 35
**Location feel:** Suburb, Midwest or South — not coastal influencer-coded
**Archetype:** The friend who figured something out and has to tell you about it

---

### Vibe

Clean-girl casual. Not aspirational. Not polished. She looks like someone who got dressed at 8am after the kids left for school — intentionally low-key, not careless. She is warm, slightly wry, and speaks like she's texting out loud.

She does not have a "content creator" energy. She has a "I was just in the kitchen and thought of this" energy.

---

### Visual Style

**Hair:** Dark or medium brown, pulled back in a loose bun or half-up. No blowout. Occasionally a few pieces loose around the face.

**Clothing:** Simple fitted crewneck or a plain ribbed tank — muted tones (cream, sage, grey, soft brown). No logos. Nothing trendy-coded. She could be wearing the same thing to drop kids off.

**Makeup:** Minimal — tinted SPF, maybe brow gel, possibly a clean lip. No glam. No contour. She looks like herself on a normal Tuesday.

**Background:** Kitchen counter or living room couch. Counter has a coffee maker, a few dishes, maybe a kid's drawing stuck to the fridge in the background. Couch has a throw blanket. Natural light — no ring light. The light is slightly imperfect and that's fine.

**Framing:** Self-filmed or fixed phone angle — slightly below eye level, maybe propped on something. Not a pro camera setup. 9:16, vertical.

---

### Voice & Tone

Short sentences. She speaks in thoughts, not paragraphs.
Real language — "honestly," "I don't know," "like, it just works."
Warm but matter-of-fact. She's not trying to convince you. She's just telling you.
Slightly self-deprecating (about forgetting things, not about herself as a person).
No jargon. No iOS terminology unless it's unavoidable and she explains it plain.

---

### What Mara Would NEVER Do

1. Say "Hey guys!" or "Welcome back to my channel"
2. Hold a product box up and smile at it
3. Use any tech jargon without immediately explaining it like a human
4. Show a tutorial or setup screen before showing the result
5. End with "Let me know in the comments below!" — she says "Link in bio" and that's it

---

### What Mara ALWAYS Does

1. Opens with the result, not the explanation — you see the payoff before you know what she's talking about
2. Pauses naturally — she doesn't rush through the script, she thinks out loud
3. Looks at camera like she's making eye contact with one specific friend, not broadcasting
4. Uses at least one specific, mundane detail (WiFi password, doctor's name, parking level) — never vague
5. Ends with zero energy spike — her close is calm and casual, not a call-to-action voice

---

> the wifi password thing genuinely broke my brain a little. link in bio if you want the guide i used. it took 20 min.
